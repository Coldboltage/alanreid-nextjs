### The SEO Push

The functionality to make sure a product page at least is populated with with at least one listing was sorted. With that, the product pages are functional and I'm happy to show them to the internet in an alpha state (technically a pre-alpha). While there's lots of SEO strategies out there, I'm doing product-led SEO which I think has been a great idea. I'll be aiming to extract as much SEO potential from the site itself. Per like how I optimised Cardsbolt before having to scale, I'm going to do as much as I can with Cardsbolt so it has every reason to rank on it's own merits. Therefore title and description optimisations were looked at and I got to a very good place with it. Given the nature of each page only updating when they need to, the title and description updating I feel is a lovely touch.

#### JSON-LD structured data

It occured to me the pages I was making might not be very understood with a search engine. While the pages are rendered statically via ISR, the table itself is not easy for a bot to read. I did add some text to make it really obvious what was the best page for that product, but I remember years and years ago about structured data. I think with Generative engine optimisation, structured data is pretty good and that was part of the motivation. As I was saying, I want to give the site every reason to rank before looking to backlinking, and it felt like Structured Data would be a good place. While it's not a ranking factor, I'm going to assume that if a page can be understood, that itself has to make a play. If not, GEO wise, it's very useful. Right now it's applied only to the product pages. I'll add it to the brand pages in the future.
