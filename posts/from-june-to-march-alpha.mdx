### AI Agents and Codex

Now I've read and saw videos of people getting applications done in One Shot and I have the feeling with Claude, this might be a lot easier but with Codex, I assume the same thing could be done. In my experience though, having it just do one job at a time on low reasoning, as it's just design, which is subjective to me, there was collabaration. I could have simply prompted it to do x-y-z and make it fix things, but I decided to go halfway with the approach. I felt Codex wasn't guessing what I was interested in but I at least knew what I didn't like and tried to make something I did like. Not very well done, but well enough in code, that it was able to start figuring plans to change things up. This collabaration I think is a good idea and allows for things to operate quickly.

Some videos I've watched speaks about the developer being taken out of the loop completely, where the full application can be made via prompting. I think there's a place for that but I'm reminded of a story my father told me. I have no idea if it's a true story or it's made up, but to be quick, it was about a small but clever enginner that understood the Ford assembly line.

> The small enginner retired or was let go, time went by and something happened to the factory where the assembly line had to stop. Lots and lots of enginners attempted to fix the problem, but no one could. Someone then said to fine said small enginner who was able to fix the problem within 15 mins. When asked about his rate, he billed them for $50,000. For 15 mins work? But the gravity of his expertise versus the potential loss of revenue without it, made this a good deal.

Something tells me you actually have to understand the system you're making because while you're able to make it now, smaller/medium applications, I believe down the line, it becomes very hard to maintain and you get to a point where specific needs becomes very hard to implemend or the code itself was unmaintainable, even for an AI. I'll need to research this more and more, but as of right now, I do not think it's a good idea to outsource the main workload to AI. While others explains being an enginner now is about orchestration, I think know enough about the underlining code you're operating with outside of the abstractions you've accepted (Nestjs, Nextjs and TS in my case), is acceptable. You at least still have an understanding and handle of the code. It's not just about understanding the code you see infront of you but the system and thus the code which should be operated upon. I don't buy it that you don't need to know that anymore.

I can look through all of the marketplaces at one time, including my list of shops, which gives me more of an edge versus many other websites which aren't doing that. I'm fairly sure I can charge a heavy fee on this because if they really want it, they'll pay it. For notifications, that's the same thing, you can pay for notifications also to check when a product is in stock including the price of it. I can do this at the users tax rate so that helps a lot. I'll have to think of the pricing structure as a product being in stock is one thing. I better bulletpoint it for my own needs.

- Notification if a product is in stock.

### TCG Marketplace costs

So if you want Cardsbolt to do the heavy lifting and find the individual product in question, we can do that. The other end of the stick is shops don't do a great job in selling from their websites, thus have to sell in an external marketplace like TCGPlayer or Cardsmarket. Now these shops usually have their own website and it would be cheaper to sell on it. Therefore I'm technically already serving them a good service because as long as their products are sold at a reasonable price, they'll get sold from the shop. They'll also not have to worry about PPC costs also because that gets expensive, though my idea might be too much to help but I'm still brainstorming this out. What I could do though is host all the products from the shop, be it cards, boxes, bundles, cases and so on, on my site. The difference is, if the page exists, I'll link to it. This eliminates the need of a middle man exchanging monies and also costs in relation to selling. People will go to the sites for the shops natively and buy from there, which should be cheaper while being the best experience. Now there's some technical hurdles because I don't want to be scrapping for millions of products so we have to go about it the Observer Listener pattern. Thankfully I've got a little bit of experience on this.

#### Shop adds code

This is a very very basic idea so stay with me. I give websites a certain API key which identifies who they are. Most likely an ID and that's about it. Whenever the shop updates the product on their site, which would be the price or available state, a PATCH call is fired to my API or an API. I'm not sure. In the patch, it'll have something in the body which gives the product SKU or SKU name. Then it'll give the price and availability. Really it can be one or another or both. With the shopId and productSku, I can then know which shopProduct to look at, which webpage to update and then update.

Now there's still the idea of setting this up where I'll need to look at their product inventory but again, they could send a POST with an array and because I have their shop, in very crude, if the product exists already in Cardsbolt, we populate the shopProduct. If not, the shop uses the productService and creates a product, it confirms the product is real via an API call to maybe OpenTCG (I would sponsor that service at this point), creates the product but only for that shop. Usually when I create a product, it's for every shop but in this case, the product is not a default product for Cardsbolt until defined (I'll need to add that oh boi). Now the product is made with the shop, we get the price and state and we're good to go once again.

That's the outline. I already grasp most of these shop owners aren't web developers so getting them to do this is a bit of a laugh. The only way I can make this work, is to create the plugins for Shopify, Woocommerce, Wix and other ecommerce platforms. They then just need to add the plugin with an API key and I do the rest. This also means I have to add some form of domain name to my API. There's always something. The idea though is with this plugin, it's able to look at the products, and pipe into the process of when a product is being updated, that I'm notified.
