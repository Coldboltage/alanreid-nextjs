I haven't posted as much as I was doing where I was posting every single day. I'm going to go back to that. While it's small victories, I think there's something fun in it.

### What more needs to get done?

While there's lots that still could be done to Cardsbolt, I think for the most part, it's pretty much ready in all aspects. There's still some problems here and there but with some tinkering, things get fixed. Yesterday when adding products, there were small issues I hadn't thought about and so, testing is probably going to be added so the behaviours of the system is put into place. That said, I did fix it and the pipeline to get products into shops and have them automatically search, while keeping things as efficient as possible, seems to be established. The website updates per change of webpage, the list easy enough to operate with and people know within a few seconds, what's the cheapest and not.

A lot of my time is in the thinking of how to better the experience and get it to a point where it's pretty obvious and I think it's now come to that point. While I'm only working on Boxed products for now, where eventually I'll look to add packs and bundles, by the end of this week, I will have that many products on Cardsbolt, the question will be asked again to myself, what else needs to be done?

As more shops and more products get added, the conversion system which allows users to know what page is cheapest for them works very well, i'm very happy with this. I've added release dates to the products so to sort them by year which makes things a little easier for future users. I think the list page itself is good enough now and it's responsive to the point, it works well on my mobile. I'll keep updating it just to make sure, but I'm pretty happy with it. I haven't added the search bar yet and made the site pretty, but that won't be long. The search bar isn't as important to me anymore. I'll get it added soon but it's pretty quick to find the stuff one is interested in. So I've pretty much done what I need to do and the question is, what's next?

### A madness, what actually is next?

Design is certainly going to have to be worked on but if I have to give bullet points, it's this.

- Add more Magic of the Gathering products
- Get One Piece on the site
- Add a few more shops and think of an agentic method which allows for ChatGPT mode to get the information I need.
- More Pokemon Products.
- Generate SEO ready meta
- Look at the SEO Strategy and Business Plan and see how that's progressed.
- Get 404 pages out of webpages asap

These feel like things I want to get all done today.

### The general fallback systems are established

The funny thing about Cardsbolt and the idea of Productsbolt, was to make it as generic as possible so one system can be applied to all the sites. I believe this has been a great success and while there are small complications thanks to rate limiting and 403 issues, scale will take care of this and for how cheap it is, I could simply just deal with this. The cost to get compute and also the resources I have myself combined with the optimisations made to Cardsbolt, makes it very easy for me to expand versus those small issues. For some of the sites which use Cloudflares custom policies, 403 issues do come up. What I have noticed though is these sites leave a lot of access available to access their products. I've already done this with Shopify sites, but make an adapter for a few more sites that use specific CMS to manage said shops, thus getting around more 403 issues. For now, cheap scaling seems to help.

### Doing the work that only needs to be done

I look at the logs and I still see lots of 404 issues. Previously I was okay to keep them because shop owners aren't exactly great at managing their products on a site. The page could be used to bring up a product, thus I didn't want to miss out on that. After lots of reviewing, I think it's safe to remove pages which are 404 and if the page ever comes up, the CRON will pick it up after 2 hours. I might not be as cutting edge as before, but something doesn't sit well with me when I'm hitting 404 pages. I'm sure i'll figure something out for that. This feels like a fun challenge to get sorted.

### Is this a doomed SaaS?

As I've said before, this project will eventually be able to go into maintance mode, which I think will be really cool. I've read a lot on Reddit, how lots of developers are building awesome things, but they don't get people on the site and they abandon the project. It's easier than ever to get the projects out there but I think that's exactly it. It's easy to make a brick wall, but to make a really good brick wall that'll last, is something else. With Skyscanner Plus, Factsbolt, Jobsbolt and now Productsbolt, I've learnt it's about making something that'll last the test of time. There's a lot of effort that goes into that and while vibecoding is awesome (something I'm yet to do), I think there's something about slowing it a bit down and allowing yourself to catch up with your thoughts. When I write my ideas down on paper, I can think clearly. When typing, I'm a bit faster but I'm still here. I have the feeling with vibecoding or agentic coding, unless you're really on the ball, the actual idea of how the whole project is going to work, the reasons to the whys and stuff, aren't fleshed out.

For the majority of us, I'm fairly sure being removed from the process of creation, takes us away from being so critical about the things that we're making. Is writing the syntax and getting the loops sorted, actually helping with the project itself. I argue it does. I think being involved in the inner workings, regardless of the abstraction of the code, allows us think. If one is still able to do it outside of the confines of the IDE, all to those people, but I don't think I'm one of them.

I'm very confident this will be a long term project which I'm able to put a few hours a week to in the future. I'm still not too sure where the money is going to come from or if it'll ever make a dime. However I'm very proud that it'll operate in such a cheap manner, it doesn't really matter. Therefore time is on my side, and I think that's wonderful. Is this doomed? I don't think so.

### What's next?

I've wrote in the bullet points what I want to get doing next and I want to see how long it'll take to get it sorted. While the idea of marketing and being out there to push the project to others swims in my mind, I want to prove the concept that Google and other search engines, including AI search engines, will just use my site as a portal to get the information required. If that's all my website is to be, I think that confirms something. That people are just using it to get the cheapest thing they want and they don't exactly need to interact with the other parts of the site. I guess that's a little disheartening, that they'll never know that it was Cardsbolt that got them to that point. I hope we get to a point where credit where credit is due, is made obvious. I hope people will be able to use Cardsbolt to find the best page for them.
